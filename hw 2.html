INDIAN CARROT PUDDING

About

Gajar ka halwa, also known as gajorer halua, Gajrela, Gajar Pak, and Carrot halwa is a carrot-based sweet dessert pudding from the Indian subcontinent. It is made by placing grated carrots in a pot containing a specific amount of water, milk and sugar and then cooking while stirring regularly. The dessert is traditionally eaten during all of the festivals in India, mainly on the occasion of Diwali, Holi, Eid al-Fitr and Raksha Bandhan which is why I love it the most as it brings out the unity of the country. It is served hot during the winter.

Ingredients (1cup = 240ml)

  • 1/2 kg carrot
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 4 cardamom
  • 2 tablespoons ghee

Optional Ingredients

  • mixed nuts (pista, cashew, almonds)
  • 2 tablespoons raisins

Preperation

  1. grate carrots 🥕
  2. dry roast nuts, also add raisins and fry until they plump up
  3. bring milk to boil and add grated carrots 🥛+🥕
  4. add sugar and stirr well
  5. add ghee and sautee the grated carrots in it 🧈
    • when it's done , the moisture evaporates and the pudding turns aromatic and thickens ♨️
  6. sprinkle cardamom powder
  7. cook for another 2-3 minutes 🥧
  8. garnish with nuts
  9. serve warm or serve chilled with ice cream 👩‍🍳

It is fragrant and rich, but you can eat bowls and bowls of the stuff. The way the warm milk coats the tongue, and how the carrots become luscious, giving off a slight taste of earth amidst all the sweetness. There are other types of halwa made with semolina or mung bean but gajar ka halwa is the most iconic
